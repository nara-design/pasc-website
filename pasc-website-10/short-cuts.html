About Short Cuts

Short Cuts is an initiative funded by the New Zealand Film Commission specifically for Pan-Asian practitioners. Short Cuts is designed to deliver a series of workshops and discussions over 2.5 days to nurture and strengthen Pan-Asian projects and/or teams.

What: Short Cuts development lab

Who: Two our of three key creatives must be of Pan-Asian heritage

When: Workshops 26-28 May 2023

Where: Auckland

Applications closed

Overview

The objective of Short Cuts is two-fold: firstly, to empower new and emerging Pan-Asian filmmakers to develop their scripted short films, and to significantly advance applications for production grants from other initiatives such as NZFC/Script to Screen’s Fresh Shorts, Outlook for Someday and NZ On Air.

Scheduled on the final weekend of May 2023, the Auckland-based lab will address topics relevant to writers, directors and producers of short films. The development lab is free and fully-catered for the selected teams, and will feature sessions from experienced New Zealand filmmakers.

PASC wants to support teams to take on leadership roles, initiate content, and contribute to the growth of Pan-Asian representation in the screen industry. PASC Executive and Short Cuts Convenor, Dr Shuchi Kothari, will be joined by other senior practitioners to deliver the syllabus.

“It’s wonderful that Short Cuts returns after a one-year hiatus. The initiative assists new and emerging teams to develop their short films in a culturally safe yet rigorous environment. It also enables one team to receive up to NZD 25,000 in production funding to realise their vision” says Dr Kothari. “At the end of Short Cuts everyone should minimally have a well-workshopped project for pitching to other initiatives or to produce themselves via crowdfunding.”

PASC thanks the New Zealand Film Commission for supporting the Short Cuts development lab. PASC also thanks its private donor and the New Zealand Film Commission for sponsoring the production funding grants.
