Membership

 

Our collective mission

The Pan-Asian Screen Collective (PASC) supports emerging and experienced screen practitioners of Pan-Asian heritage in Aotearoa.

Our goal is for Aotearoa’s cultural landscape to honestly and equitably reflect Pan-Asian faces, creativity, expertise, experience and history on screen and behind the camera.

We advocate for fairness, representation and equity in funding, and support the development of career pathways in the screen industries.

Who can become a PASC member?

Aotearoa New Zealand Screen industry practitioners or those actively studying towards a screen career in Aotearoa.

Members are all of Pan-Asian heritage—people of Asian and Middle Eastern descent.

Why become a PASC member?

Access to a vibrant community of Pan-Asian practitioners set to transform the screen landscape.

Opportunities to participate in events that specifically address the needs of Pan-Asian professionals.

Networking with like-minded professionals.

Career development opportunities via pathways that PASC facilitates.

What does it mean to be part of a collective?

Make the most of PASC events and training opportunities.

Take feedback questionnaires and surveys seriously. What you say guides strategy and planning.

Join the collective

Our job is to work with Aotearoa's screen sector to understand and value the diversity of experiences and stories that Pan-Asian New Zealanders offer.

We will have succeeded when we see and hear more of our many communities’ stories and storytellers in the broader and evolving narratives of Aotearoa.

Your membership is essential for our success in achieving this goal.

Share your successes

If you have benefited from PASC initiatives, keep us informed of your professional development and successes. Tangible outcomes allow PASC to tell funders that their investment in PASC is shifting the dial towards equitable representation.
